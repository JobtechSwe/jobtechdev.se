Jobtechdev.se Accessibility statement

Arbetsförmedlingen ( Swedish publice employment service) is the owner to this website. We want everyone to be able to use the website, regardless of needs. Here we report how jobtechdev.se complies with the law on accessibility to digital public service, and known availability issues and how you can report deficiencies to us so that we can remedy them.

How accessible is the website?

We are aware that parts of the website are not fully accessible. In the section content that is not available, we list known shortcomings in accessibility.

Contact us if you find more shortcomings

We are constantly striving to improve the accessibility of the website. If you discover problems that are not described on this page, or if you believe that we do not meet the requirements of the law, let us know so that we know that the problem exists.
Leave comments on digital accessibility

Contact the supervisory authority

The authority for digital administration is responsible for supervising the law on accessibility to digital public services. If you are not happy with how we handle your comments, you can contact the Digital Administration Authority and tell them.

Technical information about the accessibility of the website

This website is partially compliant with the Digital Public Service Accessibility Act, due to the shortcomings described below.

Content not available

  • The tab order is unclear on the Source code page.
  • There are some problems with screen readers, mainly repetitions.

How we tested the site

We have done a self-assessment (internal testing) of jobtechdev.se

How we work with digital accessibility

We strive for Arbetsförmedlingen's websites to be perceived, managed and understood by all users, regardless of need or disability and regardless of which aids you use. We must at least achieve basic accessibility by following WCAG 2.1 at level AA. Work is underway to review the Swedish Public Employment Service's digital guidelines. To give our employees the right conditions and knowledge of digital accessibility, we place great focus on educating those who develop our digital services.
